A University of Florida student, Sara was a standout athlete and played for the women’s volleyball team, which reached the last four in her senior year. Below are details on her life, career and everything in between.

Sara Sidner Biography

Sara Sidner was born on May 31, 1972 in MiamiLakes, Florida to an African American father and a British mother. Aside from her parents’ ethnicity, other details about them, family background and Sara’s upbringing are kept on everyone’s lips. She attended Hialeah-Miami Lakes High School and then enrolled at the University of Florida, where she majored in telecommunications. While at university, she played on the women’s volleyball team and helped her team reach the last four in her senior year.

Sara had just left university, passionate and determined to make an impact in the world of journalism. So she moved to WUFT-TV in Gainesville, where she started her career as an on-air reporter. With the notoriety and experience she gained from her first job, Sara Sidner moved to KFVS-TV in Cape Girardeau, Missouri, where she had a short but impactful career. Her next stop was at WINK-TV in Fort Myers, Florida. After a few years, she moved to KDFW and served as a reporter and announcer for KDFW-TV in Texas. Here she has been doing what she enjoys with a committed sense of responsibility for three years. During her time at KDFW-TV in Dallas, one of her standout stories is her coverage of the Space Shuttle Columbia disaster.

By 2004, Sara Sidner became a weekend co-host for the KTVU in Oakland, California. She has co-hosted KTVU Channel 2 News on 6 and The Ten Oclock News while also serving as a weekly news reporter for the channel.

Your career at CNN

Her efforts in the aforementioned outfits didn’t go unnoticed; Sara’s career took on another dimension in 2007 when CNN came to her service. She joined the prestigious media outfit as an international correspondent and has been posted to Jerusalem, Abu Dhabi and New Delhi, India. She currently works at the network’s Los Angeles office as Senior International Correspondent.

Sara has distinguished herself on CNN after covering the 2011 Libyan Civil War and the launch of India’s first unmanned warfare, including the Lunar Probe and the Mumbai terrorist attacks. Overall, she was brave and showed a high level of professionalism.

She has received several awards for her professionalism. A regional Emmy Award winner, Sara has won numerous Associated Press (AP) awards and more.

The 5ft 10in journalist has been equally rewarded in terms of wealth. She has made a good fortune as her net worth has been estimated at $3 million.

Who is the husband or partner?

It is no longer news that the broadcaster is married. On March 23, 2015, she hinted that she was in a married relationship on a talk show when she revealed that her busy work schedule as an international correspondent was one of the biggest challenges in her marriage. She further stated that her wedding plans were made over the phone while she was in Africa covering the Libyan civil war. Sara laughed as she added that her husband teased her to make sure she made it to their wedding.

Anyway, Sara Sidner is married, but information regarding her marriage, the identity of her husband and what they have is not known to the public. The media practitioner does not want her affairs to enter the media space. Sara just wants the public to identify with her profession and nothing more.
