Why is SFSU mascot the Gators?

Answer

The Gator, the institution’s mascot, was originally used in 1931, when the school was founded. A student newspaper, the Bay Leaf, put up a plea for a mascot, and students responded with the “alligator,” which they thought represented power and tenacity. In homage to the Golden Gate, the students also proposed the spelling “Golden Gaters,” which includes the letter “e.”
