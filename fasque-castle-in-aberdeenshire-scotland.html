Fasque Castle in Inverlochy, Scotland

Image courtesy of Fasque Castle

Accommodation

Set in the middle of private forested parkland in north-east Scotland, this Victorian castle – an exclusive-use venue – is one of Scotland's grandest and most historically significant houses.

In the 19th century it was the family home of the four-time British Prime MinisterWilliam Gladstone, and was considered the most sophisticated building in the country at the time. Now Fasque, a dramatic sandstone building in the style of a castle with spectacular public rooms and 19 lavishly furnished bedrooms, is synonymous with grandeur, luxurious interior design and complete privacy.

Image courtesy of Fasque Castle

Whether you're hosting a wedding, corporate event or family gathering, Fasque Castle is perfect for big groups. The classically decorated Great Hall leads up a sweeping double staircase into a relaxed and spacious drawing-room, library and billiard-room. The walls showcase bold colour choices (purple, deep plum and turquoise), there are elaborate wallpapers, touches of velvet and restored period furniture.

Image courtesy of Fasque Castle

Image courtesy of Fasque Castle

All of Fasque's bedrooms are finished to the very highest of standards, combining traditional luxury with a modern twist. Each has a Nespresso machine, Arran Aromatics toiletries, Egyptian-cotton bedlinen and bathrobes. There is also a charming serviced apartment in the old walled garden.

Country pursuits such as clay-pigeon shooting, fishing, horse-riding or wildlife-spotting walks can all be arranged by the quietly attentive staff.

Location

Fasque Castle is situated halfway between Dundee and Aberdeen at the foot of the Grampian hills. There is Fettercain and the Royal Lochnagar Distillery nearby, the fairy-tale Crathes Castle is a short drive away and several local golf courses are at the neighbouring Brechin, Letham Grange, Stonehaven and Montrose – all boast fine fairways, magnificent views and a good challenge for golfers of all levels.
